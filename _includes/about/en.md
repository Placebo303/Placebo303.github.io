# About Me

I am a Master's student in Optical Engineering at the [Wuhan National Laboratory for Optoelectronics (WNLO)](https://wnlo.hust.edu.cn/), [Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST)](https://www.hust.edu.cn/), advised by [Prof. Xiang Cheng](https://www.wnlo.hust.edu.cn/info/1087/1177.htm). My research focuses on high-dimensional quantum key distribution (HD-QKD) and time-frequency entanglement characterization for quantum communication networks.

## Research Interests

My research centers on leveraging the time-frequency degrees of freedom of photon pairs to enhance quantum communication systems. Specifically, I work on:

- **High-Dimensional Quantum Key Distribution (HD-QKD)**: Exploring high-dimensional encoding using time-frequency modes to increase key rates and improve noise resilience in quantum communication
- **Time-Frequency Entanglement Characterization**: Developing methods for Joint Temporal Intensity (JTI) and Joint Spectral Intensity (JSI) analysis to characterize SPDC photon pair correlations
- **Franson Interferometry**: Implementing Franson-type interference measurements for quantum state characterization and Bell test experiments
- **DWDM Frequency Multiplexing**: Utilizing Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing for parallel quantum channel encoding
- **Quantum Communication Network Protocols**: Studying entanglement-based protocols for scalable quantum networks

## Current Research

My current research revolves around high-dimensional quantum key distribution based on the time-frequency degree of freedom. The key focus areas include:

1. **DWDM Frequency Multiplexing**: Leveraging Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing to encode quantum information across multiple frequency bins
2. **Franson Interferometry**: Implementing unbalanced interferometer setups for nonlocal quantum interference measurements
3. **Time-Frequency Correlation Analysis**: Developing JTI/JSI characterization methods for SPDC source optimization
4. **Security Performance Analysis**: Evaluating Parameter Information Efficiency (PIE) and Secret Key Rate (SKR) under high-dimensional encoding schemes

## Research Motivation

The goal of this research is to address the challenge of limited coincidence events in long-distance quantum communication. By increasing the information content carried per successful detection event through high-dimensional encoding, we aim to explore more efficient and scalable quantum communication solutions.

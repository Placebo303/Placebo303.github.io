## Research Overview

My research focuses on high-dimensional quantum key distribution (HD-QKD) using the time-frequency degrees of freedom of photon pairs. The central goal is to address the challenge of limited coincidence events in long-distance quantum communication by increasing the information content per successful detection event.

## Primary Research Areas

### 1. High-Dimensional Quantum Key Distribution (HD-QKD)

Traditional QKD systems encode information in two-dimensional quantum states (qubits). My research explores high-dimensional encoding using time-frequency modes, which offers:

- **Enhanced key rates**: More bits per photon pair
- **Improved noise tolerance**: High-dimensional states are more robust against certain attacks
- **Better spectral efficiency**: Leveraging the natural high-dimensionality of photon pairs

**Current Focus**: Implementing HD-QKD systems using DWDM frequency multiplexing and time-bin encoding.

### 2. Time-Frequency Entanglement Characterization

Accurate characterization of time-frequency entanglement is essential for optimizing quantum sources. My work involves:

- **Joint Temporal Intensity (JTI)**: Measuring temporal correlations between photon pairs
- **Joint Spectral Intensity (JSI)**: Measuring spectral correlations for SPDC source optimization
- **Schmidt Number Estimation**: Quantifying the effective dimensionality of entanglement
- **Bidirectional Frequency Correlation (BFC)**: Analyzing frequency correlations in both signal and idler channels

**Applications**:
- Source quality assessment
- Entanglement dimensionality optimization
- Protocol performance prediction

### 3. Franson Interferometry

Franson interferometry provides a powerful tool for probing time-frequency entanglement through nonlocal quantum interference. My research includes:

- **Bell test experiments**: Verifying quantum nonlocality
- **Time-bin entanglement measurement**: Characterizing temporal coherence
- **Interferometric stability**: Maintaining phase stability for long-duration measurements

### 4. DWDM Frequency Multiplexing

Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing (DWDM) enables parallel quantum channel encoding:

- **Multi-bin encoding**: Using multiple frequency bins for high-dimensional QKD
- **Channel isolation**: Minimizing crosstalk between adjacent channels
- **Scalability**: Extending to higher dimensions with standard telecom components

### 5. Security Performance Analysis

Evaluating the security of HD-QKD protocols involves:

- **Parameter Information Efficiency (PIE)**: Quantifying information leakage
- **Secret Key Rate (SKR)**: Calculating achievable key rates
- **Finite-key analysis**: Security bounds for practical implementations
- **Noise modeling**: Impact of channel noise on security

## Experimental Setup

Our experimental platform includes:

- **SPDC Source**: Periodically poled lithium niobate (PPLN) waveguide for photon pair generation
- **DWDM Module**: Telecom-grade wavelength division multiplexers
- **Time Tagger**: High-resolution timing electronics for coincidence measurement
- **Franson Interferometer**: Unbalanced fiber interferometers for time-bin measurements
- **Single-Photon Detectors**: InGaAs avalanche photodiodes for telecom-band detection

## Research Motivation

In long-distance quantum communication, the key rate is fundamentally limited by the channel loss. As distance increases, the number of successful coincidence events decreases dramatically. High-dimensional encoding addresses this challenge by extracting more information from each successful detection event, effectively increasing the key rate without increasing the source brightness or reducing the channel loss.

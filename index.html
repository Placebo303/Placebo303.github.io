About Me

I am a Master's student in Optical Engineering at the Wuhan National Laboratory for Optoelectronics (WNLO), Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST), advised by Prof. Xiang Cheng.

My research focuses on high-dimensional quantum key distribution (HD-QKD) and time-frequency entanglement characterization for quantum communication networks. I am particularly interested in leveraging the time-frequency degrees of freedom of photon pairs to enhance quantum communication systems.

Research Interests

  • High-Dimensional Quantum Key Distribution (HD-QKD): DWDM frequency multiplexing, time-bin encoding
  • Time-Frequency Entanglement: JTI/JSI characterization, Schmidt number estimation
  • Franson Interferometry: Nonlocal quantum interference, Bell test experiments
  • Quantum Communication Security: PIE/SKR analysis, finite-key bounds
